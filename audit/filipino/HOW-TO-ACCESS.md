# How to open the SpellGame Filipino audit build

*One page. Works on a phone or a computer — no App Store, no install.*

## 1. Open the link
Go to **https://audit.spellgame.net** in any browser (Safari, Chrome, etc.).
It works on a phone, tablet, or computer. You can add it to your home screen if
you like, but you don't have to.

## 2. Sign in with your email + a PIN
The site is private, so it asks who you are:
1. Enter your email: **xavierguevarra06@gmail.com** (only this address is allowed).
2. You'll get a **one-time PIN** by email. Enter it on the page.
3. That's it — you're in. Your sign-in lasts **30 days**, so you can come and go
   without logging in again during the audit.

If the PIN email is slow, check spam, or request a new code.

## 3. Filipino is already selected
When you arrive, **Filipino is the active language** — you don't have to set
anything. A one-time banner at the bottom of the screen reminds you of this;
tap the ✕ to dismiss it (it won't come back).

## 4. Where the language menu is (in case you want to compare with English)
- Near the **top of the play screen** there is a small **chip** that shows the
  current setup — it reads something like **"Filipino · Easy · Untimed."**
- **Tap that chip.** A little panel slides up with three rows: a **🌐 globe** row
  (language), a **🎚 slider** row (difficulty), and a **⏱ timer** row.
- The **🌐 globe row is the language menu.** Open it and you'll see
  **"Filipino · AUDIT"** pinned at the very top — the **AUDIT** badge marks the
  language you're reviewing. English and the others are below it.
- Pick **English** any time you want to compare wording or behaviour, then switch
  back to **Filipino · AUDIT**. Your last choice is remembered.

## 5. Have fun breaking it
Play the modes, try Little Spellers (Kid Mode) in Settings, and — most
importantly — try to sneak bad words into **My Words** (Section 1 of the
checklist). Nothing you do here affects the real app or other players; this is a
private sandbox.

Record what you find in **AUDIT-CHECKLIST-FILIPINO** (the checklist file, in Word
or plain-text form). Thank you!
