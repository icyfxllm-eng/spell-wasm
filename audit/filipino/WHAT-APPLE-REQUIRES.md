# What Apple requires — and why your audit matters

*One page, plain language. This explains why the checklist focuses on the
profanity filter, Kid Mode, and the wording of the interface.*

SpellGame is on the Apple App Store, rated **4+** (suitable for everyone,
including young children). Before Apple lets a new language go live, the app has
to satisfy a few of Apple's rules. Your native-speaker review is how we prove we
meet them for Filipino. Here are the three that your audit covers.

## 1. User-generated content must be filtered  *(Apple Guideline 1.2)*
Any app that lets users type in their own content has to **filter objectionable
material** and keep it out. In SpellGame, the "My Words" feature lets a user
paste their own spelling list — that's user-generated content. Apple expects a
child (or a prankster) to be **unable to load profanity or slurs** into a kids'
app.

**Why the checklist asks you to attack the filter:** we need a Filipino speaker
to actively try to smuggle in Filipino profanity, slurs, and sneaky spellings
(Section 1). Every bad word that slips through, and every innocent word that gets
wrongly blocked, is something we fix before Apple sees it. This is the single
highest-value part of your review.

## 2. Content must fit the 4+ age rating
Because the app is rated 4+, **everything a young child can reach must be
appropriate** — the word lists, the spoken audio, and especially **Kid Mode**
("Little Spellers"). A 4+ app can't contain crude, scary, sexual, or violent
content, and can't surprise a child with purchases or outside links.

**Why the checklist asks about Kid Mode and the word tiers:** you're checking
that no Filipino word in any tier — and nothing in the friendlier Kid Mode pool —
would be unsuitable for a 4-year-old (Sections 2 and 5).

## 3. Localization must not mislead
If we tell Apple (and users) the app is available in Filipino, the **Filipino
must be real and correct** — natural wording, right spelling, no half-translated
screens, no English pretending to be Filipino. Apple rejects apps whose claimed
localization is machine-mangled or incomplete.

**Why the checklist asks about wording and pronunciation:** you're confirming the
interface reads naturally, the words are spelled in standard Filipino, and the
spoken audio actually sounds Filipino (Sections 3 and 4).

---

**In short:** your sign-off tells us — and lets us tell Apple — that Filipino
SpellGame filters bad content, is safe for a 4-year-old, and is genuinely,
correctly Filipino. That's exactly what turns Filipino from "coming soon" into a
language real families can play.
