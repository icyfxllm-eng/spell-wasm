Spell — Privacy Policy

Effective 10 July 2026

Spell is a spelling game published by the maker of spellgame.net. We built Spell to be private by design. This policy explains, in plain language, exactly what happens to information when you use the app or the website.

The short version: Playing Spell requires no account and collects no personal information — your progress stays on your device. The only time we collect personal data is if you choose to create an account to compete on the online leaderboard ("The Climb"). We never show ads, and we use no third-party analytics or tracking.

Playing the game — no data collected

You can play Spell fully without an account. Your gameplay — streaks, statistics, settings, custom word lists, and language choices — is saved only in your device's local storage. It stays on your device, is never sent to us, and is removed if you delete the app or clear its data.

What the app sends to our server to play

To play a word, the app asks our server (spellgame.net) for that word's pronunciation audio and dictionary definition. Only the dictionary word is sent — never anything about you. The app talks to our server and to nobody else: it makes no requests to any other company, and your browser is never asked to. Our server generates and caches audio for common words (using Google Cloud Text-to-Speech), and looks up definitions from dictionaryapi.dev — both by word, never by user, and both cached so the app loads quickly. All traffic uses HTTPS. We keep standard, short-lived server logs for security and reliability; they are not used to identify or profile you.

The Climb — optional accounts & leaderboard

"The Climb" is an optional online leaderboard. It is the only part of Spell that collects personal information, and only if you create an account. If you never create one, none of the following applies to you.

When you create a Climb account, we store:

  • Username and display name — shown publicly on the leaderboard.
  • Email address — used to verify your account and to reset your password. Not shown publicly.
  • Phone number — only if you choose to add one for SMS password reset. Never shown publicly.
  • Password — stored only as a salted bcrypt hash. We never store or log your actual password.
  • Your leaderboard results — your best word chain per difficulty, when you achieved it, and basic run details (e.g. word count and duration) used to rank fairly and detect cheating.

We use this information only to run the leaderboard: to sign you in, show and rank scores, verify your email, reset your password, prevent abuse, and let players report inappropriate usernames. We do not sell or share it, and we do not use it for advertising.

Service providers. To send verification and password-reset messages we use an email provider, and to block automated sign-ups we use Cloudflare Turnstile (an anti-bot check that receives your IP address to score the request — we do not store your IP address). These providers process the minimum needed to perform their function.

Delete your account any time. The app has a "Delete account" option that permanently removes your account and all associated data — profile, leaderboard entries, and sessions — from our database. You can also email us to request deletion.

Microphone and camera

The optional handwriting feature recognizes letters you draw on your own screen; the drawing is processed on your device and is not uploaded. Spell does not record audio or use your camera.

Notifications

If you turn on the daily practice reminder, it is scheduled locally on your device. We do not run a push-notification server and cannot send you messages.

Children's privacy

The game itself collects no personal information from anyone, including children. Creating a Climb account requires an email address and is intended for players old enough to have one; children playing in Kid Mode do not create accounts, and no personal information is collected from them. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we will delete it.

Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles information, we will update this page and revise the date above. Material changes will be reflected here before they take effect.
