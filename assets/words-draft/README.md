# Draft word banks — AUDIT ONLY, NOT FOR PRODUCTION

These are **unverified drafts** loaded only by the `audit_preview` build so native
speakers can play the languages and review the words. They are **never compiled
into the production app** — `tier_for` returns them only under the `audit_preview`
cargo feature; the default build serves an empty bank (a registered language with
no verified content serves no words).

## Provenance & licence
Generated by `scripts/build-draft-wordbanks.py` from public frequency corpora:
- **ar / fa / ur / ru** — OpenSubtitles 2018 (hermitdave/FrequencyWords), **CC BY-SA 4.0**.
- **hi** — Leipzig Corpora Collection (hin_news_2020_100K), **CC BY 4.0** (a
  monolingual news corpus; the subtitle lists are tiny and code-mixed for Hindi).

Both are copyleft/attribution-derived content — a draft pending the §4 decision in
DECISIONS-PENDING.md, not something to ship.

## What was validated mechanically (deterministic)
- **Attested** — every word appears in the frequency corpus (a real word, in use).
- **Typeable** — every codepoint is reachable on that language's declared keyboard
  charset (`assets/keyboards/<lang>.json`). This gate rejected 17k Persian words
  mis-encoded with Arabic yeh; the drafts are folded to correct Persian orthography.
- **NFC-normalised**, length 2–12 letters, tiered by spelling difficulty (letter
  count) among the most frequent words.

## What a native auditor MUST still do
Nothing here judges: **naturalness, register, and kid-appropriateness.** The
frequency top is dominated by function words (particles/pronouns), and subtitle
corpora carry proper nouns and adult vocabulary (e.g. Hindi surfaced words like
"kidnapping"/"hatred"). These pass every mechanical gate and must be cut by review.
Treat every word here as a proposal awaiting a yes/no.
